Data standard for Covid-19 testing

This data standard can help states and regions ensure consistency about Covid-19 tests across government agencies, vendors, and technology platforms.

Partners

This work was developed in partnership with Rhode Island's Department of Health, Department of Administration, and Office of the Governor.

Its model forms use the U.S. Web Design System, and its occupation taxonomy is based on the O*Net Standard Occupation Categories (SOCs), which are sponsored by the U.S. Department of Labor. The development and documentation of this standard has been led by The Policy Lab at Brown University.

Types of data

We've selected the fields for this data standard to minimize the administrative burden of completing a test, with additional areas selected to improve capacities for analysis, contact tracing, and testing operations.

Priority areas include:

  • Structured fields for personal information such as name, date of birth, address, and contact information,
  • Structured categories for symptoms, work settings, occupations, and places that may have been exposed,
  • Information about insurance providers to support the long-term costs of testing, and
  • Inclusive options for gender identity, sexual orientation, and housing status.

In a given state or region, you may choose to replicate this standard completely, or select sections to reuse or adapt based on your current needs.

How to read this standard

This standard provides technical documentation for developers and data analysts. For a preview of what this form looks like online, check out our model form.

For a higher-level overview of how this standard can fit into a state or region's testing operations, check out our overview section.
